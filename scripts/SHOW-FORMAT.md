# Journeyman Show — Format Guide

## Tone
Direct. Practitioner register. No performance, no hype, no "welcome back." You talking the way you talk when you're explaining something to someone who does the work.

Matches the voice on petersalvato.com and joineryschool.com. Straight to the thing.

## Episode Types

### Brand Walks (solo, 20-30 min)
You pull up a brand's web presence and walk through what you see. Screen sharing + on camera. The brand walk essays on petersalvato.com are the source material, but the episode is not a reading of the essay. It's you looking at the site live, pointing at things, saying what you notice and why it matters. The essay is the prep. The episode is the performance of seeing.

### Connections (solo, 20-30 min)
Two things from different worlds, same structure underneath. These come from the Engineering Intent material. On camera, possibly with slides or a whiteboard. You're making a connection between domains and showing why it matters for how people think about creative work.

### Conversations (guest, 40-60 min)
Real talk with someone you know. Loose structure: a few questions prepped, but the conversation goes where it goes. Two people on camera. Cut when it's done.

## Structure (all types)

**No intro segment.** No music, no bumper, no "hey guys." Start talking.

**Cold open:** One sentence that says what this episode is about. Then go.

**No outro segment.** When the idea is done, stop. Final thought if you have one, then end.

## Visual

- On camera for all types
- Screen sharing for brand walks (pull up the site live)
- Slides/whiteboard optional for connections
- Split screen for conversations (Riverside)

## Production

- Record in Riverside
- Publish to Transistor (or Buzzsprout for now)
- Strip audio for podcast feed
- Cut 1-2 clips (60-90 sec) for LinkedIn/Instagram/YouTube Shorts
