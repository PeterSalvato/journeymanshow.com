# EP03 — Connection: IEPs and CLAUDE.md Are the Same Document

## Format
On camera. Possibly a whiteboard or simple slides showing the two documents side by side. No screen sharing required but could help.

## Runtime
20-30 minutes

## Source
This is from the Engineering Intent material. The cross-domain pairing: "Classroom to AI — IEPs and CLAUDE.md are the same governance document."

---

## Cold Open

I spent seven years writing IEPs in a special education classroom in Brooklyn. Now I write CLAUDE.md files that govern how AI tools behave. They're the same document. I'm going to show you why.

## Beat 1 — What an IEP Actually Is (~5 min)
On camera. No jargon.

- An IEP is an Individualized Education Program. Every student in special education has one.
- It's a legal document that specifies: what this student needs, how you're going to provide it, what success looks like, and what accommodations are in place.
- It governs the behavior of every adult who works with that student. The classroom teacher, the speech therapist, the aide, the substitute. Everyone reads the IEP. Everyone follows it.
- The student doesn't change to fit the system. The system changes to fit the student.
- That's accommodation design. I didn't have a name for it then. I just did it.

## Beat 2 — What a CLAUDE.md Is (~5 min)
On camera.

- A CLAUDE.md is a governance file for an AI coding assistant. It tells the model: here's the project, here's how we work, here's what you're allowed to do, here's what you're not.
- It specifies: voice rules, technical constraints, positioning intent, privacy boundaries, what to never fabricate.
- It governs the behavior of the AI across every session. Different conversations, different tasks, same rules.
- The AI doesn't get to decide how to behave. The governance document decides.

## Beat 3 — The Structural Match (~8 min)
This is the core of the episode. Show the parallels.

- **Both are governance documents for a worker who can't hold context on their own.** A substitute teacher walks into a classroom cold. An AI starts every session cold. The IEP/CLAUDE.md is how you transfer intent without being in the room.
- **Both specify accommodations, not instructions.** An IEP doesn't say "teach this student math." It says "this student processes information this way, so structure the work this way." A CLAUDE.md doesn't say "write code." It says "this project works this way, so follow these constraints."
- **Both protect against drift.** Without an IEP, a substitute defaults to whatever they normally do. Without a CLAUDE.md, the AI defaults to generic behavior. Both documents prevent the worker from reverting to a default that doesn't fit.
- **Both are living documents.** An IEP gets updated when the student's needs change. A CLAUDE.md gets updated when the project evolves. Neither is written once and forgotten.
- **Both fail the same way.** A bad IEP is too vague to be useful. A bad CLAUDE.md is too vague to govern anything. "Be helpful" is as useless as "support the student's learning." The specificity is the point.

## Beat 4 — Why This Matters (~5 min)
On camera. This is the thesis.

- The skill I use to govern AI behavior is the same skill I built writing IEPs for twelve-year-olds in Brooklyn. The room changed. The skill transferred.
- Most people building AI governance frameworks are coming from engineering or policy. They're building rules for systems. I'm building accommodations for a worker who needs structure to do good work. That's a fundamentally different starting point.
- This is what Engineering Intent is about. The same structural skill shows up in different rooms across a career. Construction, print, classroom, enterprise, kitchen, AI. The transfer is the insight.

## Close
When you see an AI governance problem as an accommodation problem, different solutions become obvious. You stop asking "how do I control this tool?" and start asking "what does this tool need from me to do its job?"

That's the connection.
