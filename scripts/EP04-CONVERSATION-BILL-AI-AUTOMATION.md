# EP04 — Conversation: Bill (AI Automation at Cluen)

## Format
Two people on camera. Riverside split screen.

## Runtime
40-60 minutes

## Context
Bill works with you at Cluen. He handles automation and is working on MCP/AI integration. He publicly endorsed your GEO strategy and shares your "context desert" thesis from the product side. He sees AI from the implementation/automation angle. You see it from the governance/design angle. Same company, different lenses on the same problem.

Do NOT name the company or product on the episode. Keep it to "the enterprise platform I've worked on for 12 years" or "our product." Bill needs to agree to this boundary before recording.

---

## Cold Open (you, on camera)

I've worked on one enterprise platform for 12 years. My colleague has been building the automation layer. We see AI from completely different angles, and we keep arriving at the same problems. This is that conversation.

## Opening Question

**"What does AI automation actually look like on a product that's been around for 20+ years? What are you trying to solve?"**

Let Bill describe the landscape. He's building agents and automation on top of a mature platform. The constraints are different from a startup.

## Thread 1 — The Context Desert (~10 min)
This is the shared thesis.

- The product has decades of institutional knowledge. The AI tools have none of it.
- Ask Bill: **"When you plug an AI into our system, what does it not know that it needs to?"**
- Your angle: this is the same problem as a substitute teacher walking into a classroom. The context isn't in the tool. It's in the people who've been there.
- The point: most AI implementation fails not because the AI is bad, but because nobody figured out how to transfer context.

## Thread 2 — Automation vs. Governance (~10 min)
Where your perspectives diverge.

- Bill builds the automation: what can we make the AI do?
- You build the governance: how do we make sure it does it right?
- Ask Bill: **"When you build an automation, how do you know if the output is good enough?"**
- Your angle: "good enough" is a design question, not an engineering question. The person who decides what good looks like is doing governance work whether they know it or not.

## Thread 3 — What Breaks (~10 min)
Real examples from working on the same product.

- Ask Bill: **"What's an automation you built that worked technically but failed in practice? What went wrong?"**
- Share your own example if you have one. The design system, a generated piece of content, something where the output was correct but wrong.
- The point: the gap between "technically works" and "actually works for the user" is where design and governance live.

## Thread 4 — Who Should Lead AI Adoption (~10 min)
The positioning question.

- Ask Bill: **"In a company like ours, who should be driving AI adoption? Engineering? Product? Design?"**
- Your thesis: design practitioners should lead because they're trained to see the gap between intent and execution. Engineers build what works. Designers build what's right.
- Let Bill push back on this. The disagreement is the content.

## Close
No formal wrap. When the conversation finds its natural stopping point, stop.
